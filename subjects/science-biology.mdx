Science is about understanding how and why things work, not just memorising what they are. StudyFlow's AI tutor approaches every science question with that in mind — walking you through the underlying processes, cause-and-effect relationships, and reasoning that connect facts into real understanding. Whether you're preparing for a biology exam, balancing chemical equations, or working through a physics problem set, the AI meets you where you are and builds from there.

## Subjects covered

StudyFlow supports the following science disciplines:

- **Biology** — from cell biology to ecosystems and everything in between
- **Chemistry** — elements, compounds, reactions, and quantitative chemistry
- **Physics** — mechanics, energy, waves, electricity, and modern physics
- **Earth Science** — plate tectonics, rocks and minerals, the rock cycle, natural disasters
- **Environmental Science** — climate change, biodiversity, pollution, sustainability

## How StudyFlow explains scientific concepts

Rather than presenting information as isolated facts, the AI structures science explanations around **processes and causality**. For example, instead of telling you that the mitochondria produce ATP, it explains the sequence of events in cellular respiration and why ATP is needed in that context. When you ask about a chemistry reaction, the AI describes what's happening at the particle level. This process-first approach helps you reason through unfamiliar exam questions rather than relying purely on recall.

## Lab reports and experiment questions

StudyFlow can help you plan, write, and review lab reports. You can ask for help with:

- Writing a clear hypothesis and prediction
- Identifying independent, dependent, and control variables
- Structuring your method in a logical sequence
- Interpreting results and writing a conclusion that refers back to your hypothesis
- Suggesting sources of error and how to reduce them

You can also describe an experiment you're about to run and ask the AI to explain the underlying science, which strengthens your report's introduction and discussion sections.

<Note>
  StudyFlow provides educational guidance on experiments and lab procedures, but it is not a substitute for in-person supervision. Always follow your school or university's safety guidelines, wear appropriate protective equipment, and consult a qualified teacher or lab supervisor before carrying out any practical work. Never rely on the AI for advice about handling hazardous materials.
</Note>

## Asking about diagrams and images

Upload a photo of any science diagram — a cell cross-section, a circuit diagram, a food web — and ask the AI what it shows. StudyFlow will identify the components, label their functions, and explain how they relate to each other. This works well for textbook diagrams, past-paper images, and your own hand-drawn notes. For best results, take the photo in good lighting and make sure the whole diagram fits within the frame.
