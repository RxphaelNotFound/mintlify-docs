Language and literature sit at the heart of almost every subject you study — from writing a history essay to reading a chemistry paper. StudyFlow's AI tutor gives you dedicated support for both English (literature and language) and a growing range of foreign languages, helping you strengthen your writing, deepen your reading comprehension, and build real fluency rather than just memorising vocabulary lists.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="English">
    ## English literature

    StudyFlow helps you engage analytically with the texts on your reading list. You can ask the AI to:

    - **Explain themes and motifs** — for example, how power and control function in *1984*, or what the green light symbolises in *The Great Gatsby*
    - **Analyse characters** — their motivations, development, relationships, and how the author presents them
    - **Unpack specific quotes** — paste any line from a text and ask for a close reading, including language techniques, tone, and context
    - **Discuss context** — historical, social, and biographical context that shapes how a text should be read
    - **Compare texts** — ask the AI to help you draw comparisons across two or more works on themes, structure, or characterisation

    The AI works with a wide range of texts commonly studied at secondary school and university, including plays, novels, poetry, and short stories.

    ---

    ## English language

    For English language coursework and exams, the AI can help you with:

    - **Grammar and punctuation** — subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, comma usage, apostrophes, sentence structure
    - **Essay writing** — planning an argument, structuring paragraphs using frameworks like PEE (Point, Evidence, Explain) or PEEL, writing strong introductions and conclusions
    - **Comprehension** — paste a passage and ask the AI to help you identify the writer's purpose, tone, and use of language features
    - **Vocabulary and style** — find more precise or varied word choices, improve sentence variety, and tighten overly wordy phrasing

    ---

    ## Essay planning and writing feedback

    Upload or paste a paragraph you've written and ask StudyFlow to review it. The AI will give you specific, actionable feedback — pointing out where your argument is unclear, where evidence is unsupported, or where a grammar issue affects your meaning. You can ask for feedback on structure, style, grammar, or all three at once.

    <Warning>
      StudyFlow is here to help you understand and improve your own writing — not to write assignments for you. Submitting AI-generated text as your own work is a form of academic dishonesty and may have serious consequences at your school or university. Use StudyFlow to learn, review, and grow as a writer.
    </Warning>

    ## Translation and vocabulary

    You can ask StudyFlow to translate words, phrases, or full sentences in either direction — for example, from English to French or from German to English. For vocabulary building, ask for:

    - The meaning of a word in context (not just the dictionary definition)
    - Example sentences using the word at your level
    - Related words, synonyms, or antonyms in the target language
    - Vocabulary lists for a specific topic (e.g., *"Give me 15 Spanish words related to travel"*)

    ---

    ## Grammar rules and conjugation

    The AI explains grammar in plain language — no jargon unless you want it. You can ask for:

    - **Conjugation tables** — paste a verb and get a full conjugation table across tenses and persons
    - **Grammar rules** — ask why a particular form is used, with examples that match your proficiency level
    - **Error correction** — write a sentence in the target language and ask the AI to correct it and explain each change
    - **Pronunciation guidance** — the AI provides phonetic guides in text form to help you understand how words sound

    ---

    ## Language learning tips

    - Start each study session by asking the AI for a short vocabulary quiz on the topic you're revising.
    - When you make a grammar mistake, ask *why* it's wrong — understanding the rule stops you from repeating the error.
    - Practice writing short paragraphs in the target language and ask StudyFlow to give you feedback on grammar and natural phrasing.
