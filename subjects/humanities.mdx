The humanities ask you to think critically, construct arguments, and interpret evidence — skills that take consistent practice to develop. StudyFlow's AI tutor supports you across History, Geography, and Art by combining factual knowledge with guidance on how to structure your thinking, analyse sources, and communicate your ideas clearly in writing. Whether you're writing a history essay under exam conditions or preparing to discuss an artwork, the AI helps you move beyond surface-level recall.

<Accordion title="History">
  ## What the AI covers

  StudyFlow can help you with a broad range of historical periods and themes, from ancient civilisations to twentieth-century conflicts. Key areas of support include:

  - **Timelines and chronology** — ask the AI to outline the sequence of events leading up to or following any major event, helping you see how causes and consequences connect across time
  - **Causes and effects** — get structured explanations that separate long-term causes, short-term triggers, and immediate consequences (for example, the MAIN causes of World War I broken down by category)
  - **Key events and figures** — detailed summaries of battles, treaties, revolutions, and the individuals who shaped them
  - **Essay structuring** — ask the AI to help you plan an argument-led essay, including how to organise your paragraphs and which evidence is strongest for your thesis
  - **Primary source analysis** — paste a quote from a primary source (a speech, letter, or document) and ask the AI to help you analyse its purpose, tone, audience, and historical significance
  - **Historiography** — for higher-level students, the AI can introduce you to different historical interpretations and how historians have debated key events

  ## Asking essay-style questions

  You can pose questions directly as you would in an exam — for example, *"What caused World War I?"* — and the AI will give you a structured response organised around key points, suitable as the basis for an essay plan. Each point comes with supporting evidence and a brief explanation of its significance, so you can decide which arguments to prioritise in your own writing.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Geography">
  ## What the AI covers

  StudyFlow supports both physical and human geography at secondary school and university level.

  **Physical geography:**
  - Climate systems, weather patterns, and climate change
  - Landforms — rivers (erosion, transportation, deposition), coasts, glaciers, tectonic landscapes
  - Ecosystems and biomes — rainforests, deserts, tundra, coral reefs
  - Natural hazards — earthquakes, volcanoes, flooding, tropical storms

  **Human geography:**
  - Population — birth rates, death rates, demographic transition model, migration
  - Urbanisation — push and pull factors, urban growth, megacities, sustainable cities
  - Development — measures of development (HDI, GNI), inequalities between and within countries
  - Economic geography — globalisation, trade, transnational corporations, employment sectors
  - Resource management — water, food, and energy security

  ## Working through case studies

  Geography exams typically require detailed case study knowledge. Tell the AI which case studies are on your syllabus and ask it to summarise the key facts, statistics, and arguments you need to know — then use StudyFlow's flashcard generator to consolidate the details.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Art History and Analysis">
  ## What the AI covers

  StudyFlow's art support is designed for students studying art history, art and design, or any course that requires written analysis of visual work.

  - **Art history** — key movements and periods including Renaissance, Baroque, Impressionism, Modernism, Abstract Expressionism, and Contemporary Art; major artists and their cultural context
  - **Artwork analysis** — describe or upload an image of an artwork and ask the AI to walk you through a formal analysis covering composition, colour, line, texture, space, and how these elements create meaning
  - **Visual techniques** — understanding how artists use perspective, contrast, symbolism, and scale to guide the viewer's eye and convey ideas
  - **Contextual understanding** — how an artwork reflects the social, political, or cultural moment in which it was made
  - **Comparing artworks** — ask the AI to help you compare two works by different artists or from different movements, identifying similarities and differences in technique and intent

  ## Writing about art

  If you find it difficult to translate what you see into words, describe the artwork to the AI as best you can and ask it to model how you might write about it analytically. You can then adapt that language in your own voice.
</Accordion>

## Source analysis

Paste any quote, extract, or description of a primary or secondary source into the chat and ask StudyFlow to help you analyse it. A useful prompt structure is:

> *"This is a source from [context]. Who wrote it, for what audience, and what does it tell us about [topic]? What might its limitations be?"*

The AI will work through purpose, audience, tone, and utility — the same framework used in most humanities exams and coursework assignments.

## Creating flashcards for dates, events, and definitions

Use StudyFlow's built-in flashcard generator to turn any humanities topic into a revision deck. You can ask the AI to generate flashcards directly in the chat — for example, *"Create 10 flashcards on the causes of the French Revolution"* — and then save them to your study plan with one tap. Flashcards are especially effective for memorising chronology, geographical statistics, and art history terminology.

<Tip>
  For history revision, use StudyFlow's quiz mode to test yourself on dates and events rather than just reading them. Being forced to retrieve information — rather than re-read it — is one of the most evidence-backed methods for moving facts into long-term memory. Set a quiz on a specific period or topic and aim to complete it without looking at your notes first.
</Tip>
