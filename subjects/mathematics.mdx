## Step-by-step workings

When you submit a maths problem, StudyFlow's AI breaks the solution into numbered steps. Each step shows exactly what operation was applied and why, so you can follow the logic rather than just copy a final answer. If you get lost at a particular step, you can ask the AI to explain it further — for example, *"Why did you factorise here instead of using the quadratic formula?"* — and it will give you a direct, detailed response.

## How to enter equations

You have two options for submitting maths problems:

1. **Type the problem** using standard mathematical notation. StudyFlow understands common conventions without requiring LaTeX.
2. **Upload a photo** of a printed or handwritten problem. Tap the camera icon in the chat bar, take a clear photo of the question, and the AI will read and solve it.

<Tip>
  For best results when typing equations, use `^` for exponents (`x^2`), `/` for division (`3/4`), `sqrt()` for square roots, and `*` for multiplication. For example, type `2x^2 + 5x - 3 = 0` to enter the quadratic equation 2x² + 5x − 3 = 0. The clearer your input, the more accurately the AI can parse and solve it.
</Tip>

## Example: solving a quadratic equation

Here is how StudyFlow walks you through solving **2x² + 5x − 3 = 0** using the quadratic formula:

```text
Problem: 2x² + 5x − 3 = 0

Step 1 — Identify the coefficients.
  a = 2,  b = 5,  c = −3

Step 2 — Write out the quadratic formula.
  x = (−b ± √(b² − 4ac)) / 2a

Step 3 — Calculate the discriminant (b² − 4ac).
  b² − 4ac = 5² − 4(2)(−3)
            = 25 + 24
            = 49

Step 4 — Take the square root of the discriminant.
  √49 = 7

Step 5 — Substitute into the formula.
  x = (−5 ± 7) / (2 × 2)
  x = (−5 ± 7) / 4

Step 6 — Solve for both values.
  x₁ = (−5 + 7) / 4 = 2/4 = 0.5
  x₂ = (−5 − 7) / 4 = −12/4 = −3

Solution: x = 0.5  or  x = −3
```

You can ask StudyFlow to verify the solutions by substituting them back into the original equation, or to show you an alternative method such as factorisation.

## Tips for getting the most from maths help

- **Include all given values.** If your problem states specific measurements, constants, or conditions, include them in your question so the AI solves the right version of the problem.
- **Specify the required method.** If your teacher or exam requires a particular approach — such as completing the square rather than the quadratic formula — say so upfront: *"Solve 2x² + 5x − 3 = 0 by completing the square."*
- **Ask for a check, not just a solution.** You can submit your own working and ask the AI to identify any errors, which is a more effective way to learn than reading a model answer.
- **Request different levels of detail.** If a step-by-step solution feels too detailed or not detailed enough, tell the AI — it will adjust its explanation accordingly.
