Long, sprawling notes are hard to revise from. The Note Summariser takes whatever you've written — lesson notes, copied textbook passages, research drafts — and distils them into a clear, structured summary that highlights the key points, defines the important terms, and pulls out the main themes. Instead of re-reading five pages of notes the night before a test, you get a focused revision guide you can actually work through in minutes. It pairs especially well with StudyFlow's quiz and flashcard tools, turning a single set of notes into a full revision toolkit.

## What the Note Summariser produces

When you submit your notes, StudyFlow returns a structured summary that includes:

- **Key points** — the most important facts, arguments, and conclusions, each expressed as a clear, standalone statement
- **Key definitions** — subject-specific terms and vocabulary identified and defined in plain language
- **Main themes** — the broader ideas and concepts that connect individual facts together
- **Section headings** (if your notes contain them) — preserved and used to organise the summary logically

The output is formatted for easy reading — not a dense paragraph, but a scannable, well-structured document you can return to repeatedly during revision.

## How to summarise your notes

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Note Summariser">
    From your dashboard, click **Notes** in the left sidebar, then select **Summarise Notes**. You can also access it from the **Tools** menu at the top of any page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste or type your notes">
    Click into the text box and either paste your notes directly (from a document, website, or anywhere else) or type them in. There is no minimum length, but longer notes benefit more from summarisation. The recommended maximum is around 5,000 words per session — very long documents are best split into sections.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a summary length">
    Select how detailed you want the output to be:
    - **Brief** — a tight bullet-point summary of the highest-priority points only; ideal for last-minute review
    - **Standard** — a balanced summary with key points, definitions, and themes; good for regular revision
    - **Detailed** — a comprehensive breakdown that stays close to the original structure; useful for dense technical content like Biology or Chemistry
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the summary">
    Click **Summarise**. The AI processes your notes and returns the summary in the right-hand panel, usually within a few seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and refine">
    Read through the summary. If a section feels too sparse or a key term is missing, scroll back to your notes, add the missing detail, and click **Re-summarise** to generate an updated version.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your summary">
    Click **Save Summary** to add it to your **Notes Library**. Give it a name and assign it to a subject folder if you like. Saved summaries are available from any device.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Customising your summary length

The three length settings let you control the level of detail in the output:

| Setting | Best for | Approximate output |
|---------|----------|--------------------|
| **Brief** | Quick pre-test review, checking you haven't missed anything major | 150–300 words |
| **Standard** | Regular revision sessions, building a study guide | 300–600 words |
| **Detailed** | Complex topics, technical subjects, or when you need the full picture | 600–1,000+ words |

<Note>
  The **Detailed** setting does not reproduce your notes — it still summarises them. If you need an exact record of what you wrote, save your original notes separately. The Detailed summary is best understood as a structured restatement, not a copy.
</Note>

## Saving summaries to your account

All saved summaries appear in your **Notes Library**, organised by subject and date. From the library you can:

- **Rename** any summary
- **Edit** the text directly if you want to add your own annotations
- **Export** the summary as a plain text or PDF file for offline revision
- **Delete** summaries you no longer need

Summaries are linked to the subject you assigned them to, so they also appear in the relevant section of your **Study Plan** when you're doing revision sessions for that subject.

## Turning summaries into flashcards and quizzes

<Tip>
  The fastest way to go from passive notes to active revision is to summarise first, then generate. Once your summary is saved, click **Create Flashcards from This Summary** or **Generate Quiz from This Summary** — StudyFlow uses the key points and definitions in your summary as the source material, producing a quiz or flashcard deck that's directly aligned with what you've just reviewed. This three-step flow (notes → summary → flashcards/quiz) is one of the most effective revision patterns you can build in StudyFlow.
</Tip>

## Tips for getting the best summaries

<Accordion title="Structure your notes before pasting them in">
  Notes that already have headings, numbered lists, or clear paragraph breaks produce significantly better summaries. The AI uses structure as a signal for importance — headings and bullet points help it identify key points faster and more accurately. If your notes are one long paragraph, consider adding a few headings before summarising.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Include subject and level context at the top">
  Start your notes with a brief line like *"GCSE Chemistry — Rates of Reaction"* or *"A-Level History — Causes of World War One."* This helps the AI calibrate vocabulary and depth appropriately, especially for subjects where the same term means different things at different levels.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Split very long documents into sections">
  If you're summarising a full chapter's worth of notes, split them by sub-topic and summarise each section separately. Shorter, focused summaries tend to be more accurate and easier to revise from than a single summary of a very long document.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Use Standard or Detailed for your first pass, Brief for review">
  When you first summarise a topic, use **Standard** or **Detailed** to make sure nothing important is missed. Save that version as your main revision guide. In the days before your exam, re-open the summary and generate a **Brief** version for a rapid last-minute check — it's a fast way to confirm you still know the essentials without re-reading everything.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  The Note Summariser works on any device — paste notes from your phone, tablet, or laptop and access your saved summaries anywhere. For comfortable late-night study sessions, enable **dark mode** in your account settings to reduce eye strain while you review your revision guides.
</Tip>
