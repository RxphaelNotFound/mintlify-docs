StudyFlow's AI homework assistant is like having a patient tutor available around the clock. Instead of just handing you an answer, it walks you through the reasoning behind every problem — so you understand the *how* and the *why*, not just the result. Whether you're untangling a quadratic equation, analysing a poem, or wrapping your head around cell division, the AI meets you exactly where you are and builds your understanding one step at a time.

## How the AI chat works

Type your question into the chat box on the **Ask a Question** page, hit send, and the AI responds with a structured explanation. Each response is broken into numbered steps, so you can follow the logic from start to finish without feeling lost. The AI identifies the subject automatically, but you can also tag a subject manually if your question spans topics.

The chat is conversational — it remembers everything said in the current session, so your follow-up questions always have the right context.

## Following up in the same chat

You don't need to start a new conversation every time you have a question. Within a single session, you can:

- Ask the AI to **slow down** and re-explain one step more simply
- Request a **different method** (for example, asking for a graphical approach instead of an algebraic one)
- Say **"give me a similar example"** to see the same concept applied to a fresh problem
- Ask **"why does that work?"** to get a deeper conceptual explanation behind any step

Each follow-up keeps the full conversation context, so you never have to re-paste the original problem.

## How explanations are structured

Every AI response is divided into logical steps designed to build your understanding progressively. A typical explanation includes:

- **Identify** — what type of problem this is and what approach to use
- **Set up** — laying out the known values, formula, or structure
- **Work through** — executing each part of the solution with clear reasoning
- **Check** — verifying the answer or summarising the key takeaway

This structure means you can pinpoint exactly where your confusion starts, rather than feeling lost in a wall of text.

## Example conversation

Here's what a real chat exchange looks like in StudyFlow:

```text
You:  Solve 2x² + 3x − 2 = 0 and explain each step.

AI:   Sure! Let's solve this quadratic equation step by step.

      Step 1 — Identify the method
      The equation is in the form ax² + bx + c = 0, where a = 2, b = 3, c = −2.
      We'll use the quadratic formula: x = (−b ± √(b² − 4ac)) / 2a

      Step 2 — Calculate the discriminant
      b² − 4ac = (3)² − 4(2)(−2) = 9 + 16 = 25
      The discriminant is positive, so there are two real solutions.

      Step 3 — Apply the formula
      x = (−3 ± √25) / (2 × 2) = (−3 ± 5) / 4

      Step 4 — Find both roots
      x₁ = (−3 + 5) / 4 = 2 / 4 = 0.5
      x₂ = (−3 − 5) / 4 = −8 / 4 = −2

      The solutions are x = 0.5 and x = −2.

You:  I don't understand why the discriminant tells us about the number of solutions.

AI:   Great question! The discriminant (b² − 4ac) lives inside a square root.
      If it's positive → two real square roots → two solutions.
      If it's zero → one square root (zero itself) → one repeated solution.
      If it's negative → you'd need the square root of a negative number,
      which has no real value → no real solutions.
      Think of it as a quick "check" before you do any heavy calculation.
```

## What to do when you don't understand a step

If a specific step doesn't click, don't move on — ask directly. Phrases that work well:

- *"Can you explain Step 3 more simply?"*
- *"I don't understand what factoring means here — can you start from basics?"*
- *"Can you use numbers instead of letters to show me?"*

The AI will re-explain at a lower level of complexity, use a different analogy, or walk through a simpler version of the same problem to build up your intuition.

## Tips for asking better questions

<Accordion title="How to ask questions that get the best answers">
  **Include the full problem**
  Don't paraphrase. Paste or type the complete question, including all values, instructions, and any constraints. Missing details lead to generic answers.

  **State your subject and year group**
  For example: *"This is GCSE Biology"* or *"A-Level Maths — Mechanics."* The AI tailors the depth and terminology to your level.

  **Tell the AI what you've already tried**
  If you attempted the problem and got stuck, say so: *"I tried expanding the brackets but got a different answer."* This helps the AI pinpoint exactly where things went wrong.

  **Ask one question at a time**
  Long multi-part questions can produce unfocused answers. Break them up — ask about Part A first, then move to Part B once you understand it.

  **Use follow-ups liberally**
  The AI doesn't get impatient. If an explanation uses a word or technique you haven't learned yet, ask about that separately before continuing.
</Accordion>

## Limitations to be aware of

<Warning>
  StudyFlow's AI is a powerful study aid, but it is not infallible. It can occasionally make calculation errors, misread ambiguous phrasing, or produce an explanation that, while plausible-sounding, contains a mistake. Always cross-check important answers against your textbook, class notes, or a trusted teacher — especially for assessed work.
</Warning>

<Note>
  The AI does not complete assignments for you to copy. It is designed to explain concepts and guide your thinking. For the best learning outcomes, read each step actively and try to work through similar problems yourself afterwards.
</Note>

<Tip>
  StudyFlow works on any device — use it on your phone or tablet when you're away from your desk. If you prefer studying after dark, switch to **dark mode** in your account settings for a more comfortable reading experience during late-night revision sessions.
</Tip>
