Sometimes the fastest way to get help is to simply show the problem. StudyFlow's photo upload feature lets you take a picture of any homework question — whether it's printed in a textbook, scrawled in your own handwriting, or part of a diagram — and the AI will read it, extract the question, and walk you through a step-by-step explanation just as it would if you'd typed it yourself. No retyping long equations or squinting at small print. Just snap, upload, and start learning.

## What the AI can read

StudyFlow's optical character recognition (OCR) engine is built to handle a wide range of homework content:

  </Card>

## How to upload a photo

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Photo Upload screen">
    On desktop, click **Upload Photo** in the Ask AI page header. On mobile, tap the **camera icon** in the chat input bar. Both routes take you to the upload screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Take a photo or choose a file">
    - **Mobile:** Tap **Take Photo** to open your camera directly, or tap **Choose from Library** to select an existing image.
    - **Desktop:** Click **Browse Files** to select a saved image or PDF, or drag and drop a file onto the upload area.

    Accepted formats: **JPG, PNG, PDF** (single page). Maximum file size: **10 MB**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the upload">
    A preview of your image appears. Check that the full question is visible, then tap or click **Use This Photo** to send it to the AI.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the extracted text">
    StudyFlow displays the text it has read from your photo before generating an explanation. Check this for any OCR errors — you can edit the text directly at this stage (see below).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive your step-by-step explanation">
    Once you confirm the extracted text, the AI processes the question and returns a full step-by-step explanation in the chat. You can ask follow-up questions exactly as you would with a typed question.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Getting the best results from your photos

<Warning>
  **Photo quality directly affects accuracy.** A blurry or poorly lit image can cause the OCR engine to misread words, numbers, or symbols — which leads to an explanation of the wrong question. Follow these tips to get reliable results every time:

  - **Light the page evenly.** Use natural daylight or a desk lamp. Avoid shooting under a single overhead light that casts shadows across the page.
  - **Hold the camera directly above the page.** Keep the lens parallel to the paper — avoid shooting at an angle, which distorts text and equations.
  - **Frame the whole question.** Include every part of the question: the stem, sub-parts (a), (b), (c), any given values, and diagrams. Cropping too tightly can lose critical context.
  - **Keep the page flat.** Curved or crumpled pages cause words near the edges to blur. Press the page flat on a desk before photographing.
  - **Avoid fingers in the frame.** Fingers covering text are a common cause of OCR errors — hold the page flat from below or use a heavy object at the corners.
</Warning>

## Supported file formats

| Format | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **JPG / JPEG** | Ideal for photos taken on a phone or camera. |
| **PNG** | Best for screenshots of digital worksheets or typed documents. |
| **PDF** | Single-page PDFs only. Multi-page PDFs are not currently supported — upload one page at a time. |

<Note>
  If your homework is already digital (e.g. a PDF worksheet your teacher sent), you can take a screenshot and upload it as a PNG, or upload the PDF directly. Screenshots often produce cleaner OCR results than re-photographing a screen.
</Note>

## Editing the extracted text

<Tip>
  If the AI misreads a word, number, or symbol, you don't need to retake the photo. On the **Review Extracted Text** screen, tap any word to edit it inline. This is especially useful for correcting misread numbers in equations (for example, a "1" read as "l", or a "6" read as "b"). Make your corrections and tap **Confirm** to proceed — the AI will use your corrected version.
</Tip>

## Your privacy and photo storage

<Note>
  Photos you upload are transmitted securely over an encrypted connection and are used only to extract the question text. **StudyFlow does not store your photos beyond the current session.** Once your session ends or you close the chat, the original image is permanently deleted from our servers. Only the extracted text and the resulting explanation are saved to your account history (if you are signed in and have session saving enabled).
</Note>
