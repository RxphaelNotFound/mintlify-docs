Reading your notes is a start, but testing yourself is what makes knowledge stick. StudyFlow can generate a full quiz or a flashcard deck from any topic in seconds — or build one directly from notes you paste in. Whether you're preparing for an exam, reviewing last week's lesson, or trying to memorise vocabulary for a language class, quizzes and flashcards give you an active, measurable way to revise rather than passively re-reading the same pages.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Quizzes">
  ## Creating a quiz

  <Steps>
    <Step title="Open the Quiz Generator">
      From your dashboard, click **Quizzes** in the sidebar, then click **Create New Quiz**.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Choose your source">
      Select either **Topic** or **My Notes**:
      - **Topic:** Type a subject area (e.g. *"Photosynthesis — GCSE Biology"* or *"World War One causes"*). The more specific you are, the more focused the quiz.
      - **My Notes:** Paste in your own notes or select a saved note from your account. StudyFlow generates questions based entirely on your material.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Configure the quiz">
      Choose:
      - **Number of questions** (5, 10, 15, or 20)
      - **Question types** — Multiple choice, Short answer, True/False, or a mix
      - **Difficulty** — Foundation, Standard, or Challenge
    </Step>
    <Step title="Generate and start">
      Click **Generate Quiz**. Your quiz is ready in a few seconds. Click **Start Quiz** to begin immediately, or **Save for Later** to add it to your library.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Review your results">
      After submitting your answers, StudyFlow shows your score, highlights every question you got wrong, and provides the correct answer with a brief explanation for each. Weak areas are flagged so you know exactly what to revise next.
    </Step>
  </Steps>

  ## Quiz question types

  | Type | What it tests |
  |------|---------------|
  | **Multiple choice** | Recall and recognition — choose the correct answer from four options |
  | **Short answer** | Active recall — type a brief answer in your own words |
  | **True / False** | Quick conceptual checks — great for testing definitions and rules |

  <Tip>
    Mix question types for the most effective revision. Multiple choice alone can be misleading — a correct guess feels like knowledge. Adding short-answer questions forces genuine recall.
  </Tip>

  ## Using quizzes for adaptive test prep

  Every quiz result is saved to your **Progress** dashboard. StudyFlow tracks which topics you score consistently well on and which you keep missing, then uses that data to weight future quiz generation — surfacing more questions from your weakest areas automatically. The longer you use quizzes, the more precisely they target your real gaps.

  <Note>
    You can retake any saved quiz at any time. Retaking the same quiz after a day or two is one of the most effective spaced-repetition strategies for long-term retention.
  </Note>

  ## Saving quizzes

  Click **Save Quiz** from the results screen or the quiz library to add any quiz to your account. Saved quizzes appear under **My Quizzes** in the sidebar and can be retaken, shared, or used as the basis for a flashcard deck.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Flashcards">
  ## Creating a flashcard deck

  <Steps>
    <Step title="Open the Flashcard Creator">
      From your dashboard, click **Flashcards** in the sidebar, then click **Create New Deck**.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Choose your source">
      - **Topic:** Enter a topic (e.g. *"French vocabulary — colours and numbers"* or *"Organic chemistry functional groups"*) and StudyFlow generates a complete set of cards.
      - **My Notes:** Paste in notes or select a saved note — the AI extracts key terms, definitions, dates, and concepts to turn into cards.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Preview and edit your deck">
      Before saving, review the generated cards. You can edit the text on any card, delete cards you don't need, or tap **Add Card** to write your own from scratch.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Save the deck">
      Give your deck a name, assign it to a subject folder if you like, and click **Save Deck**. It's instantly available in your flashcard library.
    </Step>
  </Steps>

  ## Practising your flashcards

  Open any saved deck and click **Start Practice**. For each card:

  1. Read the **front** (the prompt — a term, question, or concept).
  2. Think of the answer, then click or tap **Reveal** to see the back.
  3. Mark the card as **Got it** (green) or **Still learning** (orange).

  Cards marked **Still learning** reappear more frequently in the same session. Cards you consistently mark as **Got it** are gradually shown less often, following a spaced-repetition pattern that maximises long-term retention without wasting time on material you already know.

  <Tip>
    After finishing a practice session, use **Generate Quiz from This Deck** to turn your flashcards into a short test. Switching between flashcard review and active quiz recall is one of the most effective study combinations you can build into a revision session.
  </Tip>

  ## Saving and organising decks

  Decks are saved to your **Flashcard Library** under **My Flashcards**. You can:

  - Group decks into **subject folders** (e.g. Biology, History, Spanish)
  - **Rename or delete** any deck at any time
  - **Duplicate a deck** to create a variant with different difficulty or scope

  <Note>
    Flashcard decks you create are private to your account by default. There is no public sharing — your revision materials stay yours.
  </Note>
