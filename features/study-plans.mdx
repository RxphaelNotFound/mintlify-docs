A study plan takes the guesswork out of revision. Rather than sitting down each evening and wondering what to work on, StudyFlow generates a structured, day-by-day schedule tailored to your subjects, your available time, and your upcoming deadlines. The AI breaks each subject into manageable topic sessions, spaces them out intelligently, and links directly to quizzes and flashcards so every session has a clear, actionable goal. The result is a revision routine you can actually follow — not just a timetable that gets abandoned after a week.

## What a study plan contains

A StudyFlow study plan is more than a calendar. Each plan includes:

- **Daily sessions** — specific topics to cover on specific days, timed to fit your available hours
- **Subject balance** — time allocated proportionally based on the number of topics and your exam dates
- **Built-in revision** — older topics are revisited at spaced intervals to reinforce long-term memory
- **Linked resources** — each session links to relevant AI chat topics, quizzes, and flashcard decks so you can act immediately

## Creating a study plan

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Study Plans">
    From your dashboard, click **Study Plans** in the sidebar, then click **Create New Plan**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your subjects and topics">
    Select the subjects you're studying (e.g. Maths, Biology, History). For each subject, add the specific topics you need to cover — you can type them manually or let the AI suggest a topic list based on a curriculum level you choose (e.g. GCSE, A-Level, Key Stage 3).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your exam or deadline dates">
    Enter the date of each exam or assignment deadline. StudyFlow works backwards from these dates to ensure all topics are covered with time for revision before the deadline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tell StudyFlow how much time you have">
    Enter how many hours per day (or per week) you're available to study. You can set different amounts for weekdays and weekends to reflect your actual schedule.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate your plan">
    Click **Generate Plan**. StudyFlow builds a complete day-by-day schedule and shows you a calendar view. Review the plan — if any day looks too heavy or too light, you can adjust the time allocation before saving.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and start">
    Click **Save Plan** to add it to your dashboard. Your plan appears in the **Today's Sessions** widget every time you log in, so you always know what's next.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  When setting your daily study hours, be honest rather than optimistic. A plan built around two focused hours per day is far more effective than one built around five hours you won't actually sit through. You can always add sessions manually if you find yourself with extra time.
</Tip>

## Following your plan day by day

Each day, your dashboard shows the sessions scheduled for today under **Today's Plan**. Click any session to open it — you'll see the topic, a suggested approach (read, quiz, flashcards, or AI chat), and a time estimate. Mark a session as **Complete** when you're done and it's crossed off the plan. Your overall progress percentage updates automatically.

<Note>
  You don't have to complete sessions in the exact order shown. If you want to tackle a different subject first, that's fine — just mark each session complete when you finish it and the plan stays accurate.
</Note>

## Adjusting your plan

Life doesn't always go to plan. StudyFlow makes it easy to adapt:

<Accordion title="I've fallen behind — how do I catch up?">
  Open your plan and click **Adjust Plan**. Choose **Reschedule missed sessions** — StudyFlow redistributes incomplete sessions across your remaining available days, keeping your exam dates locked. If there's too much to fit in, it flags which lower-priority topics to condense and which must be kept.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I finished a topic early — can I move ahead?">
  Yes. Mark the session complete, then click **I'm ahead of schedule** in the plan view. StudyFlow compresses the remaining sessions for that subject slightly, freeing up time you can assign to a subject that needs more attention.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="My exam date has changed — how do I update the plan?">
  Go to **Plan Settings**, find the subject whose deadline has changed, and edit the date. The plan regenerates automatically around the new timeline.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I add a new subject to an existing plan?">
  Yes — click **Add Subject** from within your active plan. Enter the new subject, its topics, and its deadline, and StudyFlow slots the new sessions into your existing schedule without disrupting what's already there.
</Accordion>

## How study plans connect to quizzes and flashcards

Every session in your plan that is tagged as a **revision session** automatically links to a quiz or flashcard deck for that topic. If you haven't created one yet, StudyFlow offers to generate it for you on the spot. This means your plan doesn't just tell you *what* to revise — it gives you a ready-made tool to do it with.

## Tips for staying consistent

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Study at the same time each day" icon="clock">
    Habit formation is powerful. Pick a fixed time slot for studying — even 45 minutes at the same time every day builds momentum faster than longer irregular sessions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Tick off sessions as you go" icon="circle-check">
    Marking sessions complete gives you a visible sense of progress. Watching the completion bar grow is a surprisingly effective motivator over a long revision period.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Don't skip rest days" icon="sun">
    Build at least one rest day per week into your time settings. Cognitive rest improves retention — your brain consolidates memories while you sleep and recover.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Review your weakest subjects weekly" icon="chart-bar">
    Check your **Progress** dashboard once a week. If one subject's quiz scores are consistently lower, consider adding an extra session for it in the following week.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
