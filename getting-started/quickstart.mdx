StudyFlow is designed to get you from question to answer as fast as possible. Once your account is set up, you can type a homework question or snap a photo of your worksheet and receive a clear, step-by-step explanation in seconds. This guide walks you through your first complete study session — from asking your first question to saving your work so you can pick it up again later.

## Your First Study Session

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the AI chat">
    Log in at [https://app.studyflow.app/login](https://app.studyflow.app/login) and click **New Session** on your dashboard. This opens the AI chat interface — a clean text input where you can type any question across your chosen subjects.

    You'll see a prompt that reads *Ask me anything…* in the message box. That's your starting point.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ask your first question">
    Type your homework question into the chat box and press **Enter** or click the **Send** button. Be as specific as you like — the more context you provide, the more targeted the explanation.

    Here are some example questions to try:

    **Mathematics:**
    > Solve for x: 3x² − 5x − 2 = 0. Show every step.

    **Biology:**
    > Explain how osmosis works in plant cells and why it matters for turgor pressure.

    **History:**
    > What were the main causes of World War I? Summarise them in order of importance.

    <Tip>
      Include your year level or exam board in your question for more relevant answers — for example: *"I'm in Year 10 studying GCSE Biology…"* StudyFlow will match the depth of explanation to your curriculum.
    </Tip>

    StudyFlow's AI processes your question and begins streaming a response within seconds.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Read the step-by-step explanation">
    StudyFlow breaks down its answer into numbered steps so you can follow the reasoning at your own pace rather than just reading a final answer. Each step explains the *why* behind each action, not just the *what*.

    For the maths example above, you'd see something like:

    > **Step 1 — Identify the equation type.** This is a quadratic equation in the form ax² + bx + c = 0, where a = 3, b = −5, c = −2.
    >
    > **Step 2 — Apply the quadratic formula.** x = (−b ± √(b² − 4ac)) / 2a …

    Take your time reading through each step. If anything is unclear, move to the next step to ask a follow-up question.

    <Note>
      StudyFlow does not just give you the answer — it shows you the method so you can apply the same approach to similar problems on your own. Use the explanations as a learning guide, not just a shortcut.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload a photo of your homework">
    If you have a printed worksheet, textbook exercise, or handwritten problem, use the photo upload feature instead of typing. Click the **camera icon** (📷) in the chat input bar, then:

    1. **On desktop** — select an image file from your computer (JPG, PNG, or PDF supported).
    2. **On mobile** — tap the camera icon to take a new photo or choose one from your photo library.

    StudyFlow scans the image, extracts the question or problem, and generates a step-by-step explanation automatically — just as it would for a typed question.

    <Tip>
      For best results, photograph your homework in good lighting against a plain background. Make sure any handwriting or printed text is clearly visible and not cut off at the edges. The AI handles messy handwriting well, but a clear image always produces a faster, more accurate result.
    </Tip>

    You can upload multiple images in the same session — useful when a problem spans two pages or includes a diagram alongside a written question.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ask follow-up questions">
    If a step doesn't click, or you want to dig deeper into any part of the explanation, type a follow-up question directly in the same chat thread. StudyFlow remembers the full context of your conversation, so you don't need to repeat yourself.

    Examples of useful follow-up questions:

    - *"Can you explain Step 3 in simpler terms?"*
    - *"What would happen if b² − 4ac was negative?"*
    - *"Give me a similar practice question I can try myself."*
    - *"How does this relate to what we covered last week on cell division?"*

    Keep asking until you genuinely understand the topic — there's no limit on follow-up questions within a session.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save your session">
    When you're done working through a problem, save the session so you can return to it later, continue the conversation, or review the explanation before an exam.

    Click the **Save** button (bookmark icon 🔖) at the top right of the chat panel. Give the session a descriptive name — for example, *"Quadratic equations — 3x² − 5x − 2"* — and click **Save session**.

    <Note>
      Saved sessions are available from the **My Sessions** tab on your dashboard. They sync across all your devices automatically, so a session you save on your phone is instantly accessible on your laptop.
    </Note>

    You can also export any session as a PDF by clicking **⋯ More options → Export as PDF**, which is handy for printing notes or sharing with a study group.
  </Step>
</Steps>
