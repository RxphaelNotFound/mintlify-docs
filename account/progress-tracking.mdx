StudyFlow automatically records every study session, quiz attempt, and flashcard run so you always have a clear picture of how far you've come. The progress dashboard pulls all of that data into one place, showing you not just how much time you've spent studying, but how well you're actually learning — and where to focus next.

## Opening the Progress Dashboard

Navigate to [app.studyflow.app](https://app.studyflow.app/login) and select **Dashboard** from the left sidebar on desktop, or tap the chart icon at the bottom of the screen on mobile. The dashboard loads your activity from the last 7 days by default.

## Switching Between Time Ranges

Use the period selector at the top-right of the dashboard to switch between views:

- **Weekly** — shows daily activity broken down by subject for the past 7 days
- **Monthly** — shows weekly totals and trend lines across a 30-day window

Your longest streak and all-time accuracy score are always visible regardless of the selected range.

## Subject-by-Subject Breakdown

The **Subjects** tab on the dashboard gives you a detailed breakdown of every topic you've worked on. For each subject you'll see:

- **Accuracy trend** — a sparkline of your quiz scores over the past 30 days
- **Time invested** — total hours studied in this subject over the selected period
- **Last studied** — how many days ago you last opened a session in this subject
- **Strength rating** — a colour-coded label (Strong, Developing, or Needs Work) derived from your recent accuracy scores

Select any subject card to open its full history, including every individual session, quiz attempt, and flashcard result linked to that topic.

## Quiz Performance History

Every quiz you complete is logged with its date, subject, score, and the specific questions you answered incorrectly. To review your quiz history:

1. Open the **Dashboard** and select the **Quizzes** tab.
2. Use the subject filter to narrow results to a single topic.
3. Select any quiz row to see a full answer-by-answer breakdown, including the correct answers for questions you missed.

This history is what StudyFlow uses to identify your weak spots and automatically increase question frequency in those areas.

## How Progress Data Personalises Your Experience

StudyFlow uses your progress data in two key ways:

- **Adaptive quiz difficulty** — questions in subjects where your accuracy is high are gradually made harder; subjects where accuracy is low receive more foundational questions until your scores improve.
- **Personalised study plans** — the AI tutor references your subject breakdown when suggesting what to work on next, prioritising topics you haven't visited recently or where your strength rating has dropped.

<Note>
  Progress-based personalisation takes effect after you've completed at least three sessions in a subject. Before that threshold, StudyFlow uses your stated difficulty preference from your profile settings.
</Note>

## Study Streaks and Why They Matter

<Tip>
  Even a short 10-minute session counts toward your streak. On busy days, open a quick flashcard deck for any subject to keep the chain alive — consistency over duration is what builds long-term retention.
</Tip>

Streaks are a proven motivational tool: the simple act of not wanting to break a consecutive run encourages you to open the app on days you might otherwise skip. StudyFlow displays your streak count on the dashboard home screen and sends you a reminder notification one hour before midnight if you haven't studied yet that day (you can adjust or disable this in [Notification Settings](/account/settings)).

## Resetting or Clearing Progress

If you want to start fresh in a specific subject — for example, at the beginning of a new school term — you can reset its progress without affecting any other subject.

1. Open the **Dashboard** and navigate to the **Subjects** tab.
2. Select the subject you want to reset.
3. Scroll to the bottom of the subject detail page and select **Reset subject progress**.
4. Confirm by entering the subject name in the confirmation dialog.

To clear **all** progress across every subject, go to **Settings → Privacy & Data → Clear all progress data**. This removes all session history, quiz scores, and streak data permanently. Your account, saved sessions, and settings are not affected.

<Note>
  Progress resets are permanent and cannot be undone. StudyFlow does not retain a backup of cleared progress data.
</Note>
