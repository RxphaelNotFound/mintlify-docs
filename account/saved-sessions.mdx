Every time you study with StudyFlow — whether that's a back-and-forth conversation with the AI tutor, a quiz run, or a flashcard deck — the session is saved to your account the moment it begins. You don't need to hit a save button or remember to export anything. When you come back later, your sessions are waiting for you in full, including every question, answer, and note from where you left off.

## How Automatic Saving Works

StudyFlow saves your progress continuously during an active session. If you close the browser tab, switch apps on mobile, or lose your internet connection mid-session, your work is preserved up to the last synced state (typically within a few seconds of your most recent action). The next time you open the app, a banner at the top of the Sessions screen will prompt you to resume any session that was interrupted.

## Viewing Your Saved Sessions

Select the **Sessions** tab from the left sidebar on desktop or the bookmark icon on mobile to open your full session library. Sessions are listed in reverse chronological order by default, with the most recent at the top. Each session card shows:

- The session title (auto-generated or renamed by you)
- The subject it belongs to
- The session type: **Chat**, **Quiz**, or **Flashcards**
- The date and duration of the last activity
- A **Resume** button if the session was not completed

## Resuming a Saved Session

To continue a session you started previously, locate it in the Sessions tab and select **Resume**. The session reopens exactly where you left off — your place in the quiz, the conversation history, or the remaining flashcard stack. For quiz sessions, your previously given answers are preserved so your score remains accurate.

## Renaming a Session

Auto-generated session titles are based on the first topic you typed, which can make them hard to tell apart later. Rename sessions to something memorable as soon as you start them.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your Sessions library">
    Select the **Sessions** tab from the sidebar or bottom navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the session menu">
    Hover over the session card on desktop to reveal the **⋯** (more options) button, or long-press the card on mobile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Rename">
    Choose **Rename** from the dropdown menu. A text field appears with the current title pre-filled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the new name and confirm">
    Type your preferred title — up to 80 characters — and press **Enter** or tap **Save**. The new name appears immediately across all views.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Name sessions after the specific topic rather than just the subject — for example, "Calculus – Integration by Parts" is far easier to find later than "Maths Session 4". Specific titles also make the search bar much more effective.
</Tip>

## Organising Sessions by Subject or Date

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Sessions tab">
    Navigate to **Sessions** from the sidebar or bottom navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a sort or filter option">
    Select the **Filter** button at the top of the session list. You can filter by **Subject**, **Session Type** (Chat, Quiz, or Flashcards), or **Date range**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply the filter">
    Select one or more options and tap **Apply**. The session list updates instantly to show only matching sessions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Change the sort order">
    Use the **Sort by** dropdown to order sessions by **Most recent**, **Oldest first**, **Subject (A–Z)**, or **Duration (longest first)**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Deleting Sessions You No Longer Need

To delete a single session, open the **⋯** menu on the session card and select **Delete**. Confirm the deletion in the dialog that appears. Deleted sessions are moved to **Trash**, where they remain for 30 days before being permanently removed. To restore a session from Trash, open **Sessions → Trash**, find the session, and select **Restore**.

To delete multiple sessions at once, select **Edit** at the top of the Sessions list, tap or click each session you want to remove, then select **Delete selected**.

<Note>
  Sessions that are permanently deleted from Trash cannot be recovered. If a session contains quiz results, those results are also removed from your progress history.
</Note>

## Sharing a Session

You can share a read-only link to any saved session with a classmate or study partner.

1. Open the **⋯** menu on the session card and select **Share**.
2. Toggle **Link sharing** to **On**.
3. Copy the generated link and send it however you like.

Anyone with the link can view the session content — including the conversation, quiz questions and answers, or flashcard deck — but cannot edit it or add it to their own account without being logged in to StudyFlow.

To stop sharing a session, return to the **Share** menu and toggle **Link sharing** to **Off**. The previous link immediately becomes inactive.

## How Long Sessions Are Stored

StudyFlow retains your saved sessions according to your account plan:

| Plan | Session Retention |
|------|-------------------|
| **Free** | 12 months from the date of the last activity in that session |
| **Premium** | Unlimited — sessions are kept for as long as your account is active |

Sessions approaching their expiry date on a Free plan will display an **Expiring soon** label on the session card, and you'll receive an email notification 30 days before deletion. Upgrading to Premium at any point before the expiry date preserves all existing sessions.

<Note>
  Deleting your account permanently removes all saved sessions regardless of plan, with no recovery option. See [Privacy & Data](/account/settings#privacy--data) for details on exporting your data before closing your account.
</Note>
